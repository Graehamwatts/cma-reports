1982 W Bayshore Rd #237 is a beautifully maintained 2-bedroom, 2-bathroom condo in the exclusive Woodland Creek gated community. This turnkey unit features an updated interior, in-unit washer/dryer, and a private balcony. Located in a 90-unit complex with premium amenities including a heated pool, spa, and fitness center, this property appeals to buyers seeking security, convenience, and low-maintenance living. The location west of Highway 101 provides a quieter setting while maintaining proximity to shopping and dining. With Menlo-Atherton High School rated 8/10 and convenient proximity to the Bay Area, this property represents excellent value in an undersupplied market.

The Market Story

The East Palo Alto condo market is extremely thin. In the last six months—a full 180 days—only one comparable 2-bedroom, 2-bathroom unit has sold in this complex. This is not a market with multiple transaction points; it's a market where individual sales matter significantly. You need to know what that single sale means and how it positions your property.

That sale was Comp #1—unit #121 in the same building, same floor plan—which sold for $765,000 on January 20, 2026, at $639 per square foot. Here's the critical insight: in March 2019, that same unit sold for $860,000. The property lost approximately $95,000 in value over six years. EPA condos have not appreciated. They've declined. This is not a growth market; it's a holding market.

What does this mean for your subject? You are pricing into a market where condo values are flat or declining, and where the most direct comparable shows that even recently updated units in the same building are struggling to hold value. The Menlo Park comps—just a few miles away—trade at significantly higher per-square-foot prices ($740/sqft and above), but Menlo Park carries a city premium that EPA simply cannot command. That gap is real and persistent.

The active listing at #217 in the same complex is priced at $799,000 ($668/sqft). It's been on the market and is directly competing for the same buyer pool. This active listing creates a ceiling. If #217 sits unsold, it signals that the market is not accepting prices above $800K for units in this building. If it sells, we get critical new data. Either way, #217's presence means the subject cannot ignore its existence—and pricing below or at parity to #217 is strategically sound.

What you have is a turnkey, updated unit in a gated community with strong amenities. That is an advantage. Your property is in better condition than average. That advantage is worth a premium over the #121 sale price—but not a large premium. The market data suggests $15,000 to $25,000 above $765,000 is realistic. Anything above $800K is speculative and depends on a motivated, non-rate-sensitive buyer willing to overlook the thin market and the flat appreciation history.

The recommendation is to price aggressively but realistically: in the $779,000 to $789,000 range. This positions your property as the better value compared to #217, signals confidence in the turnkey condition, and is fully supported by the #121 comp adjusted for quality. It gives you pricing power without overreaching into speculative territory.

Special Considerations & Risk Factors

1. Extremely Thin EPA Condo Market

Only 1 comparable 2-bed/2-bath condo has sold in the Woodland Creek complex in the last six months. The broader EPA market shows only 18 total sales across all property types in 90 days. This is an extremely limited buyer pool. Individual transactions carry disproportionate weight, and pricing power is limited. Expect a longer marketing timeline compared to more active markets. The subject must be priced realistically to stand out in this thin environment.

2. EPA Condo Values Have Been Flat / Declining

Comp 1 (#121) sold for $765,000 in January 2026. That same unit sold for $860,000 in March 2019—a loss of approximately $95,000 over six years. EPA condos have not appreciated; they have depreciated. Buyers should understand that condo values in this area are not a growth investment. Set expectations realistically and position the subject as a housing solution with strong amenities and low-maintenance living, not as an appreciation play.

3. Active Competing Listing (#217) at $799,000

Another identical unit in the same building is actively listed at $799,000. This creates direct competition for the same buyer pool. The subject's turnkey condition and strategic pricing below #217 give it an advantage, but the active listing is a ceiling test. If #217 sits unsold, it signals that the market is not accepting $799K+ pricing. Monitor #217's status closely and be prepared to adjust strategy if it sells (indicating demand) or remains unsold (indicating resistance at that price point).

4. HOA Fee of $517/Month

The HOA fee is substantial at $517 per month (~$6,204 annually). This impacts the total cost of ownership and affects buyer financing calculations. Lenders typically factor HOA into debt-to-income ratios, reducing the amount a buyer can borrow for the purchase price. Educate buyers on the total cost structure (principal, taxes, insurance, HOA) and position the HOA as justified by the gated security, heated pool, spa, and fitness center amenities.

5. Proximity to Highway 101

The property is west of Highway 101, which means potential traffic noise and air quality considerations. Some buyers will view this as a disadvantage (noise, freeway proximity) while others will appreciate the location for commute access. Acknowledge the highway proximity transparently and emphasize the gated community's buffer, the updated condition, and the appeal to Bay Area commuters who prioritize highway access over proximity to local shopping.

6. School District Ratings (Ravenswood Elementary)

The primary school option, Ravenswood City Elementary, has a low rating (2/10). This may limit appeal to families with school-age children. However, Menlo-Atherton High School is rated 8/10, which is strong. Position the property toward non-family buyers (empty-nesters, young professionals, downsizers) and emphasize the high school rating for families with older children. This is not a family-centric property; market it accordingly.
