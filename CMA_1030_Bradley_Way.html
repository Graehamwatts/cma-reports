The generous 6,499 sqft lot—larger than most comparable properties in the area—offers long-term flexibility, including potential ADU development. The Market Story

Understanding the EPA Single-Family Market in 2026

East Palo Alto's single-family market is experiencing robust activity. Over the past 90 days, 18 homes have sold with a median days-on-market of just 16 days and a median sale price of $1.08M. This tells us the market is competitive, buyers are active, and well-positioned properties move quickly. For a property like 1030 Bradley Way, this velocity matters—positioning and pricing directly determine whether you compete for multiple offers or sit on the sideline.

The strongest signal in the data is the 1024 Laurel Ave comparable. This home is nearly identical to the subject: 810 sqft, 2 bed / 1 bath, similar lot size (6,464 sqft), built 1946. It sold on January 2, 2026, for $805,950 ($995/sqft). All evidence suggests this was an as-is sale to an investor. Now, just three months later, that same property is being re-listed at $880,000. The investor recognized value in the bones and location; after minor updates, they are asking $880K. This floor-to-ceiling data tells us: as-is condition in this market is worth ~$806K. With updates and proper presentation, a home of identical specs should command $880K or better.

Graeham's own recent sales reinforce this story. The Oakwood Drive property (2 bed / 1 bath, 990 sqft, built 1959) sold March 2 for $1,035,000—$1,045/sqft. That's $229,000 more than the Laurel as-is sale, despite only 180 additional square feet. The difference was presentation, staging, and strategic pricing that generated competition. The Menalto Ave comp ($900K on Feb 12, 1,010 sqft) and the Graeham-listed Clarke Ave property ($1,130K on Mar 23, 1,280 sqft) further confirm that when EPA homes are well-presented and priced to create urgency, they consistently sell at or above asking—often significantly above.

Critically, homes priced strategically below market are generating the strongest overbids. The Illinois St comparable listed at $899,000 and sold for $955,000—a 6.2% premium. That property was 1,030 sqft with 3 bedrooms, which should command less premium than a tightly positioned 2-bed on a larger lot. The pattern is clear: aggressive pricing below the natural market level triggers competition and multiple offers, resulting in final prices above what a higher list price would achieve. This is the Graeham Watts strategy in action.

For 1030 Bradley Way, the lot size is a differentiator. At 6,499 sqft, it is larger than most EPA comparables and offers future flexibility—ADU development, additional parking, or simply the appeal of more outdoor space. Buyers in this market are increasingly thinking long-term; the lot is worth more per square foot than the home itself. Combined with the transit access to Meta and Google campuses just minutes away, this property will appeal to a diverse buyer pool willing to invest in location and future-proofing.

The path forward is clear: List strategically in the $880,000–$940,000 range to capture the broadest buyer pool, position the minor updates as completed or imminent, highlight the lot potential, and expect the market to respond with competition. Based on recent comparable sales and buyer behavior in EPA, a well-presented 1030 Bradley Way should generate offers pushing the final sale price into the $900,000–$950,000 range—consistent with comparable sales data and current market conditions.

We are confident that 1030 Bradley, in good condition with completed or imminent minor updates, should command equal to or above the Laurel flip asking price of $880K.

Pricing in the $880K–$940K range achieves multiple objectives:

1. Captures the broadest buyer pool. EPA buyers are actively searching in the $850K–$950K bracket. This range pulls in both entry-level buyers and move-up buyers, maximizing competition for your property.

2. Historical data support for overbids. The Illinois St comparable proved this strategy: listed at $899K, sold for $955K (6.2% over). Menalto, listed around $849K, sold for $900K. Oakwood and Graeham's recent Clarke listing also sold well over asking. Strategic pricing within this range consistently triggers competition that pushes the final sale price above asking.

3. Positions for multiple offers. With 16 median DOM and 18 sales in 90 days, EPA is a competitive market. A list price in the $880K–$940K range with strong presentation will attract 2–4 qualified offers within the first 10–20 days, creating a negotiation environment favoring the seller.

4. Leverages Graeham's recent track record. Oakwood (2/1, 990 sqft) sold for $1,035K; Clarke (3/1.1, 1,280 sqft) sold for $1,130K. Graeham knows how to stage and price EPA SFRs for maximum buyer competition. The market recognizes this.

5. Expected sale price of $900K–$950K. Tenants are leaving, minor updates will be completed, the lot offers ADU potential, and transit access is excellent. These are legitimate value-adds that, combined with strategic pricing, position this property for a final sale price of $900,000 – $950,000. This is consistent with recent EPA sale patterns and supported by the comparable data.

Bottom line: List at $880,000 – $940,000. Expected sale price: $900,000 – $950,000. The market determines the final price regardless of listing strategy—but listing in this recommended range gives you the optimal balance of buyer exposure, competitive tension, and market credibility to reach the top of that expected range.

Special Considerations DOM Important Factors for Listing & Marketing

⚠County Records vs. Actual Layout

County assessor records indicate 4 bedrooms; however, the actual functional layout is 2 bedrooms / 1 bathroom. This discrepancy should be explicitly addressed in the listing description and during showings. It represents either historical misclassification or a layout change that was not updated in county records. Opportunity: Frame the flexible space (den, office, bonus room) as a feature for remote workers or small businesses. Disclose clearly to avoid appraisal complications or buyer disputes post-offer.

Tenant Transition Timing

Current tenants are moving out. Coordinate listing launch to coincide with or immediately follow tenant departure. This enables professional staging, photography under ideal conditions, and buyer access for showings without tenant coordination. Consider a 2-week staging and marketing prep window after tenant move-out.

Minor Updates Impact & Timeline

Minor cosmetic updates (paint, landscaping, basic repairs, staging) will significantly enhance buyer perception and price realization. The Laurel comp's jump from $806K (as-is) to $880K (after updates) demonstrates the value. Ensure updates are completed before or immediately at listing launch. Photos should showcase the updated condition; do not list the property pre-update.

ADU Potential & Lot Value

The 6,499 sqft lot (0.15 acre) is larger than most EPA comparables and legally eligible for ADU development under current CA zoning. This is a material value differentiator, especially for investor buyers. Include zoning information in the MLS remarks and marketing materials. Consider a brief addendum highlighting ADU development potential (cost estimates, timeline, rental projections) for investor interest.

Lot-to-Home Value Ratio

The lot represents a disproportionate share of total value. At 6,499 sqft with a potential ~$5M/lot implied value (based on recent lot sales in EPA), the buildable land is worth more per sf than the 1947-built home. This is a long-term value play and appeals to investors planning renovations or future development. Position this in marketing narrative.

Tech Campus Proximity & Commute Appeal

Meta and Google campuses are 2–4 miles away (5–10 minute commute). East Palo Alto is becoming an attractive option for tech employees seeking shorter commutes, walkable neighborhoods, and home equity. This demographic is active in the $800K–$1M price range and values location and future appreciation. Emphasize commute advantage and emerging neighborhood momentum.

No Unpermitted Work

The property has no unpermitted work, which simplifies appraisals, inspections, and financing. This is a competitive advantage—explicitly highlight in marketing and offer documentation. It reduces buyer friction during due diligence.

Single-Family Buyer Demographics

2-bed / 1-bath properties in EPA appeal to: first-time homebuyers with modest down payments; small families or couples seeking entry-level SFRs; investors targeting rental or fix-flip; young professionals from tech companies. Market to all three segments with distinct messaging (first-time buyer programs, family potential, investment metrics).
